What is a Subdomain Takeover?

A subdomain takeover is a vulnerability that lets attackers claim control of a subdomain that belongs to someone else — silently, and often undetected for months. Here's how it works, why it happens, and how to protect yourself.

01 // The basics

Every website has a domain. But most websites also have subdomains — prefixes like shop.example.com, blog.example.com, or api.example.com that point to different services or parts of the site.

Companies often point these subdomains to external services using a DNS record called a CNAME. For example, a company might point their blog subdomain to a Ghost or Webflow site:

blog.example.com → CNAME → example.ghost.io ✓ configured

This works fine — until the company stops using that Ghost site and forgets to clean up the DNS record.

02 // How the attack works

When the external service is deprovisioned but the CNAME record remains, the subdomain becomes dangling. It points to an address that no longer has an owner.

blog.example.com → CNAME → example.ghost.io ✗ unclaimed

An attacker who notices this can simply register that Ghost account at the same address. Now they control what appears at blog.example.com — a subdomain that looks completely legitimate to anyone visiting it.

01
Company sets up blog.example.com → example.ghost.io and launches their blog.
02
Company migrates to a new platform and deactivates the Ghost account — but forgets to delete the CNAME record.
03
Attacker scans DNS records, finds the dangling CNAME, and registers example.ghost.io.
04
Attacker now controls blog.example.com and can serve phishing pages, steal session cookies, or damage the brand.

03 // Real world examples

Subdomain takeovers are not theoretical. They appear regularly in bug bounty reports and have been found on domains belonging to major companies.

⚠ Vulnerable — GitHub Pages
Subdomain: shop.example.com
shop.example.com → mystore.myshopify.com
The Shopify store at mystore.myshopify.com no longer exists.
✗ VULNERABLE — Anyone can register this Shopify store and claim the subdomain.
⚠ Vulnerable — Heroku
Subdomain: staging.example.com
staging.example.com → old-app.herokuapp.com
The Heroku app was deleted but the DNS record was never removed.
✗ VULNERABLE — Dangling CNAME. The Heroku app address is unclaimed.
✓ Safe — Properly configured
Subdomain: docs.example.com
docs.example.com → example.readme.io
The Readme.io project exists and is actively configured.
✓ SAFE — Service is configured, fingerprint not present.

04 // What can an attacker do?

Once an attacker controls a subdomain, they can:

→
Serve phishing pages — the subdomain looks legitimate, making users more likely to trust it and enter credentials.
→
Steal session cookies — if the main domain shares cookies with subdomains, an attacker can intercept authenticated sessions.
→
Bypass Content Security Policy — a trusted subdomain can be used to bypass CSP rules that allowlist the parent domain.
→
Damage brand reputation — serving malicious or embarrassing content under an official-looking company subdomain.

05 // Vulnerable services

Subdomain takeovers are possible on any platform that lets users register custom subdomains or custom domains. The most commonly exploited services include:

GitHub Pages Heroku AWS S3 Azure Vercel Netlify Cloudflare Pages Shopify Ghost Webflow Zendesk Freshdesk HubSpot Fastly Surge Render Fly.io Squarespace Tumblr Notion Gitbook Supabase + 60 more

06 // How to protect yourself

The good news is that subdomain takeovers are completely preventable. Here's what you should do:

01
Audit your DNS records regularly. Keep an inventory of all your subdomains and what service each one points to.
02
Delete DNS records when decommissioning services. When you shut down a service, the very first step should be removing the DNS record — before anything else.
03
Check for dangling CNAMEs periodically. Run a takeover check on all your subdomains every few months, especially after infrastructure changes.
04
Use a subdomain takeover checker. Automated tools can detect dangling CNAMEs instantly across all your subdomains at once.
